    How the federation population (695M) is calculated  method and sources
    1. Baseline: start from the EU-27 population baseline (Eurostat 2026 baseline), approximately 448 million.
    2. Add accession and candidate populations expected to be integrated into the federation by 2050 (illustrative additions used here): United Kingdom (~71M), Turkey (~90M), Ukraine (~35M), Moldova (~2.5M), Western Balkans combined (~17M), and South Caucasus / neighbouring additions (~18M). These subtotal to roughly ~233M.
    3. Apply demographic change to 2050 (fertility, mortality, and net migration) using medium-variant population projections from Eurostat and UN World Population Prospects; together these raise the enlarged-population subtotal to the rounded figure of ~695M by 2050.
    4. Notes on rounding and uncertainty: all intermediate country figures are rounded to one decimal or whole millions for presentation; final totals are illustrative and sensitive to migration assumptions, accession timing, and future demographic trends.
    Primary sources and references: Eurostat population and projections; UN World Population Prospects (WPP); national statistical offices for individual country baselines (e.g., ONS, TurkStat, UkrStat).       What this is. EU2050 reads EU policy documents and news every day, and uses that to adjust two long-run 2050 scenarios for Europe: one where integration stalls (Scenario A), and one where the EU becomes a genuine federation with a completed single market, capital markets union, digital sovereignty and an enlarged membership including the Western Balkans, Ukraine and Moldova (Scenario B).

      These are illustrative scenarios synthesised by AI from current reporting, not statistical forecasts or predictions of fact. Population figures for 20262050 are based on Eurostat baseline projections; integration, sovereignty and enlargement scores are an AI-assisted reading of policy momentum and are inherently uncertain and contestable.
