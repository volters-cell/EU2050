What this is. EU2050 reads EU policy documents and news every day, and uses that to adjust two long-run 2050 scenarios for Europe: one where integration stalls (Scenario A), and one where the EU becomes a genuine federation with a completed single market, capital markets union, digital sovereignty and an enlarged membership including the Western Balkans, Ukraine and Moldova (Scenario B).

These are illustrative scenarios synthesised by AI from current reporting, not statistical forecasts or predictions of fact. Population figures for 2026–2050 are based on Eurostat baseline projections; integration, sovereignty and enlargement scores are an AI-assisted reading of policy momentum and are inherently uncertain and contestable.
